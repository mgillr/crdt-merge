# Licensing FAQ

## What license is crdt-merge under?

Business Source License 1.1 (BSL 1.1). The full text is in the
LICENSE file at the repository root.

## What does BSL 1.1 mean in practice?

You can use crdt-merge for any non-production purpose without
restriction: development, testing, research, education, personal
projects, internal evaluation, and academic work.

For production use (defined as use in a system that serves external
users or generates revenue), you need a commercial license until the
Change Date.

## When does it become fully open source?

On 2028-04-08, the license automatically converts to Apache 2.0.
After that date, crdt-merge is free for all uses with no restrictions
beyond standard Apache 2.0 terms.

## Why BSL instead of MIT or Apache 2.0 from the start?

BSL protects the investment in developing novel algorithms (the CRDT
merge architecture and E4 trust protocol) while ensuring the code
becomes fully open source on a fixed timeline. This follows the model
used by MariaDB, HashiCorp, Sentry, and other infrastructure projects
that need sustainable commercial funding.

## What about the patents?

Two UK patent applications cover the architecture:
- GB 2607132.4: Base CRDT merge architecture (OR-Set + Merkle provenance)
- GB2608127.3: E4 Recursive Trust-Delta Protocol

The patents protect the specific novel mechanisms described in the
applications. They do not restrict use of the software under the
license terms. After the Change Date (2028-04-08), Apache 2.0 includes
an express patent grant.

## Can I contribute to crdt-merge?

Yes. Contributions are welcome under the project's Contributor License
Agreement. Contributors retain copyright to their contributions and
grant the project a license to distribute under BSL 1.1 (and
subsequently Apache 2.0).

## Can I use crdt-merge in my research paper?

Yes. Academic and research use is explicitly permitted under BSL 1.1.
Please cite the package and relevant patent applications.

## Can I fork crdt-merge?

Yes, under BSL 1.1 terms. Your fork inherits the same license,
including the Change Date and conversion to Apache 2.0.

## What is the commercial license?

Contact the maintainers for commercial licensing terms. Pricing
follows a tiered model based on deployment scale.
